Jobs for Intermediate persons

If you have completed your intermediate (12th grade) education and are looking for government jobs in Pakistan, there are a variety of options available to you. Here are some common job categories that often require an intermediate qualification:

1. Clerical and Administrative Jobs:

Clerk/Typist:

These roles involve tasks like data entry, record keeping, and general office duties.

Stenotypist/Stenographer:

These roles involve taking dictation in shorthand and transcribing it.

2. Assistant and Subordinate Staff:

Assistant/Sub-Inspector:

These positions are available in various government departments and involve assisting higher-level officials.

big>

3. Healthcare and Medical Jobs:

Nurse/Medical Technician:

Intermediate pass candidates may be eligible for certain nursing and paramedical positions in the health sector.

4. Technical and Skilled Trades:

Technician/Technical Assistant:

Various technical positions in government departments may require an intermediate qualification along with relevant technical skills.

5. Police Jobs:

Sub-Inspector/ASI:

These are higher-level positions in the police department that may require an intermediate qualification.

6. Education Department Jobs:

Secondary School Teacher (SST):

In some cases, secondary school teaching positions may be available to candidates with an intermediate qualification.

7. Banking and Financial Institutions:

Clerk/Teller:

Some positions in banks and financial institutions may be accessible to candidates with an intermediate qualification.

8. Armed Forces and Paramilitary Jobs:

Clerk/Stenographer:

In the armed forces or paramilitary forces, there are positions for candidates with an intermediate qualification in administrative roles.

9. Airports and Aviation Jobs:

Airport Security Force (ASF):

Certain positions within the ASF may be available to intermediate pass candidates.

10. Railways and Transportation:

Ticket Collector/Assistant Station Master:

Ticket Collector/Assistant Station Master:


Remember to carefully review the specific requirements and eligibility criteria for each position you apply for, as they may vary depending on the department and job role. Keep an eye on official government job portals, newspapers, and relevant websites for announcements and updates regarding job openings. Additionally, consider registering with government employment agencies for potential opportunities.
