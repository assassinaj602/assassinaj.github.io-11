Social Security

The social security system in Pakistan encompasses various programs aimed at providing financial support and protection to its citizens. The Employees' Old Age Benefits Institution (EOBI) offers assistance to retired workers in the organized sector, while the Benazir Income Support Programme (BISP) provides unconditional cash transfers to low-income families. The National Social Protection Programme (NSPP) extends aid to vulnerable groups, including widows, orphans, disabled individuals, and the elderly. There are provisions for social protection of overseas Pakistani workers, covering areas such as insurance, pension, and repatriation of bodies in case of death. The Workers' Welfare Fund (WWF) offers welfare facilities to industrial workers and their dependents, including medical care, housing, education, and recreational amenities. The Employment Injury Scheme (EIS) provides compensation for work-related injuries or accidents, and maternity benefits are granted to female workers, which may encompass paid leave, medical coverage, and related support.so for the most current and detailed information, consulting official government sources or reliable publications related to social security in Pakistan is recommended.

1. Employees' Old Age Benefits Institution (EOBI):

This program is designed to provide financial assistance to retired and old-age workers from the organized sector of the economy. It covers employees working in industries, commercial establishments, and other organizations.

2. National Social Protection Programme (NSPP):

The NSPP includes various initiatives aimed at providing social protection to vulnerable groups, such as widows, orphans, disabled individuals, and the elderly. It encompasses multiple programs, including Bait-ul-Maal and others.

3. Social Security for Overseas Pakistanis:

This program is designed to provide social protection to Pakistani workers who are employed abroad. It covers areas such as insurance, pension, and repatriation of bodies in case of death.

4. Workers' Welfare Fund (WWF):

The WWF aims to provide various welfare facilities to industrial workers and their dependents. It includes provisions for medical care, housing, education, and recreational facilities.
